Introduction

I investigate large-scale systems, services, and applications operating across the interconnected environments of cloud, edge, and NextG networks. This includes designing smart AI systems (edge AI, federated/distributed ML, generative AI), optimizing edge/cloud computing (resource/data/workload management, service delivery, emerging communication infrastructures), advancing energy systems (data center decarbonization, demand response, EV-grid synergy), fortifying cybersecurity (DDoS mitigation, anomaly detection), and innovating for multimedia experiences (VR, streaming, gaming, social networks). At its core, my work applies principles of network optimization and control, alongside tools such as learning and game theory, systematically integrating computer science with engineering mathematics. This interdisciplinary approach enables rigorous modeling, design, analysis, implementation, and experiments focused on ensuring system efficiency, scalability, sustainability, and resilience. My research has been supported by grants from the National Science Foundation, including the CAREER award, and the industry.

I am a research associate professor at the University of Oregon, working with the Center for Cyber Security and Privacy. Previously I worked at Nokia Bell Labs in Ireland. I completed my Ph.D. (Dr. rer. nat.) degree in computer science (Informatik) at the University of Göttingen (Georg-August-Universität Göttingen), Germany, where my work was part of EU projects. I received the Ripple Faculty Fellowship, the Alcatel-Lucent Bell Labs UK and Ireland Recognition Award, and the Best Paper Awards of IEEE CNS 2019 and IEEE LANMAN 2013. I was a guest editor of JSAC, and have also served in many program committee roles, including as a track chair for ICDCS, as a member for conferences like INFOCOM, MOBIHOC, ICDCS, and WWW, and as a chair for multiple workshops with INFOCOM and ICDCS.
